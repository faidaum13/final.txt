<p><font size=20 color=white>
<h2>do's</h2>
<strong>Cut:</strong>
<ul>
<li>wash hands</li>
<li>take tetanus shot</li>
<li>clean the wound</li>
<li>cover the wound</li>
<li>watch for infection signs</li>
</ul>
<strong>scald:</strong>
<ul>
<li>cool down with tapwater</li>
<li>keep warm in a blanket</li>
</ul>
<strong>dislocation of a bone:</strong>
<ul>
<li>must be put back to place as fast as possible (this should be done by a professional healthcare worker of a doctor. if not available then, must be performed by kowledgable person about medical emergencies like this)</li>
</ul>
<h2>don'ts</h2>
<strong>Cut:</strong>
<ul>
<li>don't touch</li>
<li>avoid contact with rusty and dirty materials</li>
</ul>
<strong>scald:</strong>
<ul>
<li>must not use cold water or ice for cooling down</li>
<li>do not apply any greasy substances(like butter, oil and etc.)</li>
<li>avoid scald getting rubbed on a surface(like the blanket for warming)</li>
</ul>
<strong>dislocation of bone:</strong>
<ul>
<li>don't touch the dislocated area</li>
<li>don't let the dislocated area swell and get the nerves more stretched(meaning do something as much as possible to return on its original place)</li>
<li>don't move the injured person(unless there are hazards on the place)</li>
<li>avoid too much moving</li>
</ul>
