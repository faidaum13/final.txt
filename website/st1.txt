<header><h1>Symptoms and Treatment</h1></header>
<p><font size=20>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
Cold:
<ul>
<li>dryness and hotness of skin</li>
<li>vomiting</li>
<li>headache</li>
<li>loss of appetite</li>
<li>dark urine</li>
</ul>
Chickenpox:
<ul>
<li>pain</li>
<li>burning</li>
<li>inflammation</li>
<li>blisters</li>
</ul>
Sore throat:
<ul>
<li>scratchy sensation in the throat</li>
<li>fever</li>
<li>pain when swallowing</li>
</ul>
Ear infection:
<ul>
<li>feeling sick</li>
<li>fever</li>
<li>pain in the ear</li>
<li>slight loss of hearing</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment</h2>
Cold:
<ul>
<li>nasal spray(for blocked nose)</li>
<li>drink plenty of water(to stay hydrated)</li>
<li>combination of cough and cold medicines(not recommended if the child is not that sick)</li>
<li>paracetamol</li>
<li>rest</li>
</ul>
Chickenpox:
<ul>
<li>serve sugar-free popsicles</li>
<li>apply lotion(such as: fragrance-free lotion, calamine lotion, and anti-itch lotion)</li>
<li>keep nails trimmed short</li>
<li>stay indoor</li>
<li>wear gloves or mitten</li>
<li>soft bland diet(for chickenpox that sores in the mouth)</li>
<li>chamonile compress</li>
<li>Antihistamines for itching</li>
</ul>
Sore throat:
<ul>
<li>gargle with saltwater</li>
<li>stay indoor</li>
<li>Avoid irritants</li>
<li>drinking of water</li>
<li>rest</li>
</ul>
Ear infection:
<ul>
<li>distraction(such as: playing, watching, eating and etc.)</li>
<li>sleep without putting pressure on the infected ear</li>
<li>medicine(such as: Acetaminophen, Ibuprofen and Naproxen)</li>
<li>cold, warm or both compress</li>
</font>
</ul>
